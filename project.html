  • Project Idea

    • Overview

    An idea I had in mind for the project would be an application that is directed at assisting artists with anatomy and lighting references. The goal for this app is designed for artists to have the convenience of a reference tool to be accessible wherever they are, so long as they download the app; as well as having access to high quality 3D models that they can manipulate into their desired poses. Not only are users able to customise the poses of the models themselves, but they can also set up different lighting in the scenes to gain an almost realistic reference of how lighting and shadow work.

    Motivation

    This project will be incredibly useful to have, as finding reference itself on the internet is quite a time-consuming process, not to mention having to find the exact photos to suit your ideal vision, to then start creating. With the tools intended to be designed in this program, it would not require the use and knowledge of how to use an advanced 3D program such as Autodesk's Maya - let alone earning the license to be able to access such a program, and it would greatly lessen the learning curve required to use a 3D modelling program as well as reduce the cost of time that is needed to begin drawing/modelling/sculpting.

    Description

    The 3D Models:
    High quality human models ideally with low-poly — the same kind used in a game's gameplay instead of the pre-rendered cutscenes models. The basic human models include a Male, Female and non-binary body, with two ages of either an Adult or a Child (for first phase of the app). There would also be a range of body types included with the models with the choice of a skinny, lean, curvy, overweight and muscular build. The model's skin colour is changeable, with a variety of hues to choose from. Basic household items, such as chairs, tables, cooking utensils are included in the 'v.01' app. As patches roll out for this app, there is a possible inclusion of adding either the elderly or infant phase of the human's age, as well as the inclusion of miscellanous items which are then categorised into 'themes', for example, fantasy-related objects (swords, shields, mage staff, etc.) to sci-fi items, etc.; Animals, Monsters and anthropomorphic models would also be a later addition to the app.

    The human models all come pre-rigged, so users just need to import them into the scene and begin moving the joints, setting up their desired lighting and taking screenshots. During the model selection stage, it functions like a Sim/Character creator but with only basic options. Since the idea is not for users to create their own personalised character per se, but to use these base models as reference (like the Body Kun mannequin doll figures)

    • Pre-positioned poses:
    The app would include basic poses that have been pre-positioned and saved by the devs, so new users can get started right way. These poses would simply be quite generic such as sitting, holding objects, etc. which then act as a good foundation for users to base their own custom poses on. Users would not only be limited to controlling and manipulate the joint movements of the body/limbs but also the facial expressions, and positioning of the mouth.

    The Lighting Tool:
    Users are able to add in as many lights as they want with the flexibility to control each of the light's hue, strength (how intense or soft the light — imitating either godrays, a spotlight, or ambient light), positioning in the scene as well as the angle it faces. For users with less knowledge of lighting, there will be options to select basic lighting types that have already been preset; such as basic three-point lighting, studio lighting and portrait lighting. The base source light (the sun) will always already be set in the scene with the option to disable it for night. Users will also be able to save their own lighting setups as a preset, to use whenever they want over a multitude of times.

    Screenshot Tool:
    Another available tool is the ability to capture screenshots in the form of a jpeg or png. Users can freely move around the camera to any angle of their scene and take screenshots, which then can be exported/saved. Before saving their images, there will be an available option for them to select "save as transparent" - which gives them the choice to either remove the background scene, so only the model with the lighting rendered is shown. The purpose of this is so that users can easily import their photos into their desired editing program without the hassle of having to remove the background themselves.

    The Scene:
    An empty, expansive space that acts as a background for users to place their models and lights in. This background will have almost transparent but still visible guidelines with a directional compass (X,Y,Z) to assist users in placing objects accurately in the scene.

    Tools, Technologies, Skills

    Initially the intention was for only the developers and collaborating artists to be able to import 3D models in, so the required software for them would have to create such models would be the 3D program of their choosing — could range from Autodesk Maya, 3DSMax, Z-Brush to using an open source one such as Blender. For the more industry standard 3D software, having a semi-powerful computer(at the least) is required to render those models without issues. Developers and designers would require the use of their preferred software for application creation and editing tools, whereas users need to at least have the latest versions of Windows or IOS on a computer to use the application, and preferable on Apple and Android phones as well.

    Skills Required

    > Programming language knowledge — for developers of this app to write the correct code to execute the actions required in making the mechanics function properly.

    > Design knowledge — developers need to be able to create the UI elements in a minimalistic and straight-forward way so that it's easier for users to navigate through, encouraging them to learn how to use the app quicker and without too much difficulty compared to learning how to use an actual 3D modelling program. The overall design of the app has to be clean, neatly organised and aesthetically pleasing to the eye.

    > 3D Modelling Skills — for the devs/artists collaborating with the developers, they must know how to create and sculpt 3D models from high-poly to low-poly, rig said models with the appropriate joints and skin them properly without breaking the mesh.

    > Intermediate/Expert knowledge of Anatomy — required of the artists, as this is what serves as the foundation for users to who have limited knowledge on anatomy.

    > Thorough knowledge on Lighting and Colour Theory — this applies more so to the developers/designers, to be able to set things up from the start. More basic knowledge of lighting theory is encouraged for users to be able to know how to set up lights.

    > Intermediate knowledge of how to use computers and applications— required by all users in order to navigate and use the application's tools to its full capacity.

    Finding the required skills especially the 3D artists and programmers might be a challenge, as this app sounds quite complex to program, as well as the quality intended for the models are quite high — the artists have to show their thorough understanding of anatomy and some aspects of art theory, so finding the right artists to add their work in may be costly to devs, or they may not have enough time to specifically create models for just this one app.

    Outcome

    If this project is successful, the outcome would give birth to a powerful reference tool that should be sought out by all artists and students studying in the fields of art. The results of whether this solves the original problem of increasing an artist's workflow and reducing time spent on finding references is dependent on the user themselves. Since every individual works in different ways - they have their preferred workflows, and may not be opened/fussed to learning and introducing a new segment into it, it's hard to determine how effective this app will be as a whole. As there is a small learning curve required to use the app itself, some individuals may not be willing to go through with learning how to use it. However if this app becomes a recommended tool by artists I believe it has the opportunity to grow into something more than just a 3D-mannequin-posing app but may even be able to support artists financially by uploading their models into this app.
