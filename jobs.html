    Why RMIT?

    My interest in IT sparked when learning Unreal Engine in my game art course. Due to the complex nature of the program and the steep learning curve required to use it, it opened up more features and tools that bordered between the lines of design and programming. This exposure to development features led me to pursue more knowledge about technological tools and the basics of IT. From there it led to the hardware aspects of a computer - buying parts and building a computer capable of rendering 3D art programs. Since I am still currently learning and exposing myself to most of the aspects of IT, outside of the entertainment/gaming genre, my closest relevant experience would have to be using cloud systems to efficiently share work with other team members and when using the Unreal Engine/3D programs.

    During my studies, I expect to learn basic programming skills, such as basic coding and website building skills to further add to my design/art repetoire. UX design was also something I have been particular interested in but never had the opportunity to fully study during game development, as I find it is an important skill to have and understand when designing projects. I also hope to gain experience in working with teams in developing apps and learn more development processes for personal/business-related projects for the future.

    Ideal Job

    The position of Director of Technology is responsible for planning, directing and coordinating computer-related activities within the company. They are the ones who oversee the information technology of this company, through researching and assessing new and trending technologies to implement into the current system, as well as improving currently existing development processes on client projects and the organisation as whole. They are responsible for determining the information technology goals of the company by leading multiple tech development team members; with the inclusion of providing mentorship and assistance to both the clients and the teams. The salary this role offers is a bonus in my opinion but aside from that, I'm drawn by the flexibility that it has — to be able to work within different areas of a company (e.g. web developers, designers,etc) as well as the practice of this role, from researching the latest technologies to organising the content/projects of what team members are working on — it sounds like responsibilities that correspond well with how I would like to spend my days at work doing.

    The Director of Technology is responsible for researching and assessing emerging technolgies to judge whether/how they can be implemented into the organisation's systems and client's projects. They manage the web development team as well as performing/reviewing basics of a project before the final product. As this is a 'Director' role, the qualifications needed show you have to be well-versed and experienced in programming languages and web frameworks with a background in managing teams. An expert level of software development appears to be a major requirement for this role.

    The only relatable skills I currently have relating to this position would be my skills in design and ability to build and maintain interpersonal relationships which would assist in my communication with clients as well as the development team members. Depending on what company I work for, I intend for my background in game development to play a part in this position in order to help goal-orientate the team and lead them with a clear vision.

    My current plan is to gain more knowledge learning skills in software development through full-time study in a Bachelor course. Through uni projects I intend to work with multiple teams in order to gain a better understanding of how I function in a team, what drives a team, and what processes work efficiently with team members, etc. Upon graduating or maybe even before, I would like to obtain a job in an entry level IT-related field, preferably a position that involves UI/UX design - in order to keep training and refining my artistic/design skills, to then gaining the relevant and required experience to work my way up to a position of director from there.
